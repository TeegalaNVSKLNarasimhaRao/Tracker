1. Getting started

The app is a single HTML file hosted on GitHub Pages. No installation, no app store, no subscription. Open it from any browser on any device.

  1. Open your GitHub Pages URL: https://teegalanvsklnarasimharao.github.io/Tracker/
  2. Sign in with your Google account.
  3. Your data loads automatically from the cloud and stays in sync across all your devices.
  4. Bookmark the URL or add it to your phone home screen for quick daily access.
On Android: open Chrome, tap the three-dot menu, tap Add to Home Screen. On iPhone: open Safari, tap Share, tap Add to Home Screen. The app appears on your home screen like a native app.
The app works offline after the first load. Firebase caches your data locally so you can use it without internet. Changes sync automatically when you reconnect.

2. Sign in and multiple users

The app uses Google Sign-In via Firebase. Every person who signs in gets their own completely private space — no data is shared between users.

How multiple users work

Each Google account gets its own independent data — their own schedules, habits, diary, tracker history, holidays, and settings. When Narasimharao signs in, he sees his data. If a family member signs in with their own Google account on the same device, they see their own completely separate data. Nothing is shared.

Signing in

Click Sign in with Google on the login screen. A Google account picker appears. Select your account. If the popup is blocked by your browser, the app automatically falls back to a redirect-based sign-in. After signing in, your data loads from the cloud in a few seconds.

Signing out

Click Sign out in the top bar. A confirmation dialog appears before signing out. Your data is saved to the cloud before sign-out. The next person to sign in on the same device gets a clean, fresh session with their own data.

3. Top bar

☾ Dark mode / ☀ Light mode Updated

The button shows what will happen when you click it. When you are in light mode the button shows ☾ Dark mode. When you are in dark mode it shows ☀ Light mode. Click it to switch. Your preference is saved to the cloud and remembered across all your devices and browsers.

📷 Snapshot

Generates a plain-text summary of today: your focus, water intake, habits done, and the full schedule for today. The text copies to your clipboard automatically so you can paste it into WhatsApp, Notion, email, or anywhere else. A preview also appears below the schedule.

↓ Export

Downloads all your data as a JSON backup file named habit_backup_YYYY-MM-DD.json. The file contains everything: schedules, habits, tracker history, diary entries, holidays, water logs, focus entries, and pauses. Use this as a safety backup even though your data is also in the cloud.

↑ Import

Restores data from a previously exported JSON backup file. After importing, your data is immediately saved to the cloud. Use this to migrate data from an old version of the app or to restore from a backup.

Sign out

Signs out of the app safely after a confirmation. All pending saves are cancelled cleanly. Any running Pomodoro timer is stopped. The session is reset for the next user.

5. Always-visible widgets

Today’s banner

Shows today’s full date, which schedule to follow today (auto-detected from day of week and holidays), a badge showing weekday / weekend / holiday status, your saved focus for today, and a View today button that jumps directly to today’s schedule tab.

Today’s focus

A single text field for your most important priority today. Type it and press Save. It appears in the banner and in the daily snapshot. Each day gets its own focus entry. Old focus entries are automatically cleaned up after 60 days to keep your data lean.

Water tracker Updated

Ten water drop icons representing glasses of water (250ml each, 2500ml total goal).

  • Tap any drop to set your intake to that many glasses. Tap the 6th drop = 1500ml logged.
  • Tap a lower drop to reduce your intake. If you had hit the goal (10 glasses) and reduce below 10, the water habit is automatically un-ticked in the tracker.
  • Auto-tick: When you reach 2500ml, the 2.5 L water habit is automatically marked done.
  • 7-day bar chart: Shows your water intake for each of the last 7 days. Each bar now shows the exact amount in ml or L above it.
  • Water streak: Consecutive days hitting the 2500ml goal, counted backwards from today.
  • Reset: Sets today back to zero.

Pomodoro timer Updated

A 25-minute focus countdown timer. Start it by clicking the 25m button on any purple Learning block in the schedule. The timer uses real timestamps so it stays accurate even if your phone screen turns off or you switch browser tabs. Starting a new Pomodoro while one is running correctly replaces the old timer.

7. Habit Tracker tab Updated

Track which habits you complete each day of the week. Each habit is identified by a unique internal ID so reordering or deleting habits never corrupts historical data.

The table

Each row is one habit. Each column is one day. Tap any circle to cycle through states:

  1. Empty — not yet logged
  2. Green ✓ — done
  3. Grey — — skipped intentionally

Week navigation

  • ← Prev / Next → — browse past and future weeks
  • This week — jump back to the current week instantly
  • Reset week — clears all habit data for the currently viewed week (asks confirmation)
A warning banner appears whenever you are viewing a past or future week, reminding you that any changes save to those dates, not today.

Habit notes

Tap the small + below any circle to add a note for that habit on that day. Quick chips help: Done!, Skipped - busy, Skipped - forgot, Partial done, Extra effort, Will catch up.

Month percentage

Each habit shows its completion rate for the last 30 days — more honest than just looking at one week.

Stats and streak

The streak counter shows consecutive days where every non-paused habit was completed. The streak always reflects your actual current streak regardless of which week you are browsing.

Milestones

Special celebration messages appear at 7, 14, and 30 consecutive perfect days.

8. Manage Habits tab

Add, edit, delete, reorder, and pause habits. Reordering habits never affects historical tracker data because each habit has a unique ID, not a position number.

Habit cards

Each habit shows its name, description, monthly completion percentage, and action buttons: ↑ ↓ to reorder, Edit to change name/description, Delete to remove permanently.

Adding a habit

Tap + Add habit. Enter a name and goal description. You can also add habits automatically when creating a new schedule block by selecting Also add as a habit in the block form.

Pausing a habit

Tap Pause a habit. Select the habit and enter a from/to date range. Paused days show as P in amber and are completely excluded from scores, streaks, and monthly percentages. All active pauses are listed at the bottom of this tab with a Remove button.

10. Diary tab Updated

A private daily journal. One entry per day, synced to the cloud.

Writing an entry

Use ← → arrows to navigate between dates. Tap Today to return to the current date. Select a mood, write in the text area, press Save entry.

Auto-save

The diary automatically saves your entry after 30 seconds of inactivity. The auto-save timestamp appears below the text area. If you navigate to a different date or switch tabs while typing, the entry is auto-saved immediately so nothing is lost.

Ctrl+S / Cmd+S

Press Ctrl+S (Windows) or Cmd+S (Mac) while on the Diary tab to save your entry instantly without clicking the button.

Last entry hint

If today has no entry yet, the diary title shows when you last wrote — helpful for remembering your writing habit.

13. Data, backup and cloud sync

Where your data lives

All data is stored in Firebase Firestore — Google’s cloud database — under your unique Google account ID. Data is split into three separate documents for efficiency: one for settings and schedules, one for habit tracker history, and one for diary entries.

Offline support

The app works offline after the first successful load. Firebase caches your data in the browser’s IndexedDB. Changes made offline are queued and automatically synced when you reconnect.

Real-time sync

When you make a change on one device, it automatically appears on your other open devices within seconds — no refresh needed.

Privacy and security

Firebase security rules ensure that only you, when signed in with your Google account, can read or write your data. No other user, and no one browsing your GitHub Pages URL, can access your habits or diary.

Export backup

Tap ↓ Export to download a complete JSON backup of all your data. Do this weekly as an extra safety net. Store it in Google Drive or another cloud location.

Import / restore

Tap ↑ Import and select a backup JSON file. Your data is restored and immediately saved to the cloud. Works for migrating from old versions of the app.

Data pruning

Water intake logs and daily focus entries older than 60 days are automatically cleaned up during saves. This keeps your data lean without affecting any habits, diary entries, or tracker history.

Storage usage

With daily use, the app uses approximately 500KB per year. The Firebase free tier allows 1GB of storage — enough for well over 1,000 years of daily use. You will never need to pay for Firebase with this app.

FAQ

Q: I signed in on a new device and my data is there immediately. Is that correct?
A: Yes. Your data is in Firebase. Any device you sign into with the same Google account loads your complete data automatically.

Q: I reordered my habits and my old tracker data changed.
A: This should not happen in v10. Each habit has a unique ID. Reordering, deleting, or renaming habits does not affect historical tracker data. If you see this, you may be on an old version — upload the latest index.html to GitHub.

Q: The water habit did not auto-tick even though I hit 2500ml.
A: Make sure a habit named exactly 2.5 L water exists in Manage Habits. The auto-tick looks for that exact name. If you renamed it, the auto-tick will not find it.

Q: I deleted a habit. Is the tracker history gone?
A: The tracker data (ticks and notes) remains in your database but is no longer linked to any habit. The heatmap and monthly percentages will not include it. The raw data stays and will be pruned naturally over time.

Q: How do I undo a habit tick?
A: Tap the circle again. It cycles: empty → done → skip → empty.

Q: Can I use this offline?
A: Yes after the first load. The app caches all data locally via Firebase offline persistence. Changes sync when you reconnect.

Q: My diary entry was not saved.
A: The diary auto-saves after 30 seconds of inactivity and also when you switch tabs or navigate to a different date. You can also press Ctrl+S / Cmd+S to save instantly. If you close the browser very quickly without saving, the auto-save may not have fired — always tap Save entry for important entries.

Q: What does the past/future week banner mean?
A: When you navigate away from the current week in the Tracker tab, a warning banner appears. Any habits you tick there will save to those past or future dates, not today.

Q: The sync bar shows a red dot.
A: A save failed. This usually means you lost internet connection. Your data is safe locally via offline persistence and will sync automatically when you reconnect. You can also try refreshing the page.

Q: Can other people see my diary or habits?
A: No. Firebase security rules restrict all data to your Google account only. Even someone with your GitHub URL cannot access your data — they would see the app but no data, because they are not you.
